Description :

Ansys 2024 R2 is a pivotal release that operationalizes AI, expands multi-physics system simulation, and deepens cloud-native capabilities. Building on R1's foundations, it focuses on turning innovative AI tools into production-ready solutions, enhancing collaborative workflows, and solving larger, more integrated engineering challenges.

Key Innovations & Themes:

    Production-Ready AI & ML Integration:

        Ansys SimAI Transitions to Full Release: The physics-informed AI surrogate modeling tool now offers enhanced model accuracy, broader application support (including structural mechanics and fluids), and robust APIs for integration into automated digital twin and optimization loops.

        Expanded AI+ Capabilities: Ansys Mechanical introduces AI-powered result prediction for parameter variations, suggesting likely stress and deformation trends without full solves. Fluent gains AI-guided solution strategy recommendations for faster convergence.

    Unified System Simulation & Digital Thread:

        New: Ansys System Coupling Enhancements: A more powerful and user-friendly platform for tightly coupled multi-physics simulations (e.g., fluid-structure interaction, thermal-electric). It features improved scalability, monitoring, and control for complex co-simulation workflows.

        Ansys ModelCenter & Digital Mission Engineering: Deeper integration with STK (Systems Tool Kit) and enhanced MBSE (Model-Based Systems Engineering) connectivity, enabling seamless flow from system architecture models to high-fidelity physics simulations and back.

    Cloud-Native & Collaborative Workflows:

        Ansys Cloud Direct Integration: Tighter, more transparent integration within Mechanical, Fluent, and Electronics Desktop. Features like one-click cloud solve and real-time cloud job monitoring are embedded directly into the native UI, making high-performance computing (HPC) access effortless.

        Ansys Minerva Evolution: Enhanced simulation process and data management (SPDM) with new collaborative review dashboards and knowledge capture templates, improving governance and reuse of simulation best practices across global teams.

    High-Fidelity Physics & Solver Advancements:

        Fluent: Major updates for battery simulation with new electrochemical and thermal runaway models. Continued expansion of GPU-accelerated solvers to more physical models, including combustion and acoustics.

        Ansys LS-DYNA: New composite material failure models and enhanced machine learning-based material calibration workflows for more accurate crash and impact simulation.

        Ansys SPEOS: Expanded optical sensor simulation for automotive LiDAR and camera systems, including dynamic weather and lighting effects for perception system validation.

    Usability & Interoperability:

        Streamlined Electronics Workflows: Tighter integration between HFSS, SIwave, and Icepak enables more automatic setup for electro-thermal co-analysis of PCBs and IC packages.

        PyAnsys Ecosystem Growth: Further expansion of the Python scripting and automation toolkit across all major products, empowering users to build custom applications and connect Ansys into their broader IT/PLM landscape.

Core Philosophy: 2024 R2 shifts from introducing AI concepts to embedding them into core simulation workflows and strengthening the connective tissue between system-level design and detailed physics. It emphasizes making powerful simulation more actionable, manageable, and collaborative at an enterprise scale, ensuring insights drive decisions faster.

Ideal For: Engineering organizations scaling AI/ML-driven design exploration, teams developing complex cyber-physical systems (autonomous vehicles, electrification), electronics and energy storage innovators, and IT/Simulation managers implementing cloud-based, collaborative simulation platforms.
